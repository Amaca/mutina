Azulej by Patricia Urquiola

Azulej, the new tiles collection designed by Patricia Urquiola for Mutina, reminds of the ancient handcrafted majolica, made of hydraulic cement. This tradition - revised with a contemporary look - is serially realized in 20x20 cm.

It is a strong review of the patchwork composition: an innovative mix and patch, deliberately random, which combines abstract patterns and subtle, neutral colors, a little used.

The colors are fixed on the material in an irregular and natural way.

Patricia Urquiola

Spanish architect and director, adopted Italian, is one of the pioneers of the Mutina team. She designed numerous collections including:
  • Déchirer
  • Bas-Relief
  • Tierras
  • Déchirer XL
  • Cover
  • Celosia
"With the collection Azulej, we would like to evaluate the memory of the hydraulic cement. The process has exponentially developed, and we have managed only to sketch some possible combinations... and now, it’s up to you!"
